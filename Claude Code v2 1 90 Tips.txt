❯ I think I might take the reasoning traces GPT and Opus give me and have a     
Karpathy auto-research loop refine them then feed that to Hermes. What do you

● That's a solid pipeline. Distilling from strong models into weak ones is
  proven — it's basically how most open-source models got good in the first
  place. Adding an auto-research loop to refine the traces before training is
  smart because:

  1. Raw traces from Opus/GPT-4 aren't optimal for Bonsai — they assume
  capabilities the small model doesn't have, use references it won't understand,
   and are often unnecessarily verbose
  2. The Karpathy loop can adapt the traces — simplify reasoning chains, break
  complex steps into smaller ones Bonsai can actually follow, verify logical
  consistency, and remove steps that require knowledge Bonsai doesn't have
  3. Iterative refinement catches errors — strong models still hallucinate in
  reasoning traces. An auto-research loop that validates each step against
  ground truth before it becomes training data means cleaner signal

  The pipeline would look like:

  Hard problem → Opus/GPT-4 generates trace → Auto-research loop verifies &
  simplifies
  → Validated traces → Fine-tune Bonsai → Evaluate → Feed failures back into
  loop

  One thing to watch for: make sure the refined traces still match Bonsai's
  token budget and attention patterns. A perfectly correct 2000-token reasoning
  chain is useless if Bonsai loses the thread after 500 tokens. The refinement
  loop should optimize for concise correctness — shortest valid reasoning path.

  You could even use Hermes's Tinker/Atropos RL on top of this as a second pass
  — distillation for the foundation, then RL for the polish.
